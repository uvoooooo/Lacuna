# Idea: Graphrag-based truth forensics and logical gap completion  

Have you ever experienced this: You listen to a narrative. Although the speaker is trying to illustrate what he has suffered, you discover the fact that he is actually more defective in this story. Given that his narrative is self-supportive, why can you finding that? Did you ever think about it? 

The very core of this idea is, you should not listen to what he has said, but pay more attention to what is missing in this whole picture. That is, listen to what hasn't been involved.

In technical context, I am thinking about what if: Build a deep inference engine capable of reconstructing the truth from fragmented information and filling logical gaps. It transforms unstructured public-opinion fragments collected from the web into dynamic event graphs with causal weighting. Through LLM-driven entity extraction and link prediction, the system connects scattered facts and performs conflict detection. More importantly, leveraging graph completion capabilities, it can logically extrapolate potential clues that should exist but are currently missing, enabling the identification of possible opinion reversals or concealed chains of interests within partial information.
